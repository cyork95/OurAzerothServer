Welcome to OurAzeroth

OurAzeroth is a custom World of Warcraft (Wrath of the Lich King 3.3.5a) single-player server designed for cooperative, immersive adventure. Explore a living world scaled down dynamically for solo and group play, recruit custom AI companions, converse with LLM-powered bots, and build your legend in a classic progressive era.

This wiki contains setup guides, bot control references, custom modules lists, rare spawner details, and everything you need to connect and thrive.

1. Connecting to the Server

To connect to the local server, you must edit your WoW client's configuration file to point to the server host's network IP.

Update the Realmlist file

  1. Open your World of Warcraft 3.3.5a game directory.
  2. Navigate to Data/enUS/ (or Data/enGB/ depending on your client version).
  3. Find the file named realmlist.wtf, right-click it, and open it with **Notepad**.
  4. Delete everything inside and replace it with:
set realmlist 192.168.1.168
  1. Save the file and close Notepad.
2. Setting up Borderless Windowed Mode

The original Wrath of the Lich King client does not have a native "Borderless Windowed" checkbox. Running the game in traditional Fullscreen can crash modern Windows PCs or cause issues when switching screens. Maximized Windowed mode acts exactly like borderless fullscreen.

Manual Configuration Settings

  1. Go to your World of Warcraft folder.
  2. Open the **WTF** folder.
  3. Open the file **Config.wtf** in **Notepad**.
  4. Scroll to the bottom and paste the following lines:
SET gxWindow "1"
SET gxMaximize "1"
SET gxRefresh "60"

*Note: If your monitor has a higher refresh rate (e.g. 144Hz or 240Hz), change the "60" value in gxRefresh to match your screen.*

  1. Save the file and launch the game. It will now open as a seamless borderless window.

3. HD Graphics Pack & 4GB Patch

The server is configured to load beautiful high-definition textures and models if you have installed the optional HD pack.

How to Install the HD Pack

  1. Ensure your downloaded HD pack files end with .mpq (e.g. patch-W.mpq).
  2. Move these patch files into your WoW client's **Data/** folder (NOT the enUS subfolder, directly in Data/).
  3. Important: Do not overwrite or rename files named patch.mpq, patch-2.mpq, or patch-3.mpq. Rename your custom HD patches to any unused letter or number (e.g. patch-W.mpq, patch-K.mpq, or patch-5.mpq).
  4. Delete the **Cache/** directory inside your WoW folder before launching. This folder is safely rebuilt by the game and prevents graphics glitches.
⚠️ CRITICAL: HD Graphics Crashes & The 4GB Patch

WotLK was released in 2008 as a 32-bit application. Windows strictly limits 32-bit applications to **2 GB of RAM**.

Because the HD Pack loads extremely large textures, changing your display options or walking into populated zones will quickly exceed 2 GB, causing your game to crash instantly (usually throwing **Error #132**).

The Fix: Download the free utility tool here: Download NTCore 4GB Patch Tool. Open the utility, select your Wow.exe file, and apply the patch. This allows the game to use up to 4 GB of RAM, fully preventing any HD graphics-related memory crashes.

Your AI Companions & Bot Control

Instead of playing completely alone, you can design custom companion characters who follow you, dungeon with you, and help you quest, or command the random playerbots running around the world.

1. Creating a Custom Follower

Go to the Admin Control Panel and scroll to the bottom to find the **AI Custom Follower Creator** card. Fill in your character's name, choose a name for your follower, choose their race/class/gender/level, and click **Create Follower**.

⚠️ Server Restarts & In-Memory Cache

The game server loads all character files into an in-memory cache when starting up. When you create a follower through the web dashboard, they are saved directly in the database, but the running game server won't know they exist yet.

Before you can summon them, you must restart the server. You can do this by clicking "Reload Conf" or "Restart Services" in the Admin Panel, or by typing .server restart 5 in your game chat. The server takes about 10 seconds to reboot.

4. In-Game Bot Commands Reference

To control your bot companion, you must communicate with them. You can give commands in two ways:

  • Group Chat: Type the command in party chat (e.g. /p follow) and all bots in your group will obey.
  • Whispers: Send a private whisper to a specific bot (e.g. /w BotName follow) to command only that bot.

summon

Teleport to Me: Immediately teleports the bot(s) directly to your location.

follow

Tells the bot to follow you immediately (default stance).

stay

Tells the bot to hold its position and stop moving.

flee

Tells the bot to stop fighting, ignore threat, and run directly to you.

grind

Tells the bot to wander nearby and attack any hostile creatures in range.

formation [type]

Sets group march formation. Options: near, arrow, line, shield, circle.

attack

Tells the bot to immediately attack your current target.

cooldowns

Tells the bot to use long combat cooldowns (trinkets, major abilities).

threat

Prints out the bot's current threat table details.

tank / dps / heal

Sets the combat role. For example, whisper a druid heal to prioritize healing spells.

autogear

Auto Equip: Tells the bot to scan its bags and automatically equip the best available gear.

equip upgrade

Tells the bot to specifically scan its inventory bags for gear upgrades and put them on.

s [gray/vendor]

Sell Items: When standing near a merchant, whisper s gray to sell trash, or s vendor to sell unusable gear.

repair

When standing near a repair vendor, this tells the bot to repair all its equipment.

nc -loot / +loot

Disable (nc -loot) or enable (nc +loot) looting of corpses entirely.

talents spec <specname>

Set Specialization: Changes the bot's spec preset (e.g. talents spec shadow).

talents spec list

List Specializations: Whispers back all available specialization names for the bot's class.

talents autopick

Auto-pick Talents: Automatically allocates all talent points matching their current level.

trainer learn

Train Spells: When standing near a class trainer, whisper this to have them buy/learn all available spells.

cast [spell name]

Tells the bot to cast a specific spell on your target. Example: cast Power Word: Fortitude.

spells

Lists the active spells the bot is capable of casting.

stats

Prints out the bot's current stats (Armor, Crit, Spell Power, Defense, etc.).

Talking to Bots (Ollama AI Chat Integration)

The server is integrated with a local Large Language Model (Ollama). This allows the playerbots in the game world to behave like real players, complete with unique personalities, factions, and area awareness. You can have conversations with them!

How to Chat with Bots

  • Say Chat (`/s [Message]`): Stand near a bot in the world and type in local Say chat. For example, if you stand near Dianne, type /s Hey Dianne, nice weather today. There is a **90% chance** she will reply back to you privately or in local chat!
  • Party Chat (`/p [Message]`): If you invite bots to a group, they will listen to party chat. If you say something to the group, bots have a **90% chance** to reply, joke, or chime in on your conversation.
🔒 Why Whispers Do Not Trigger AI Chat

Whisper replies are disabled for Ollama. Whispering a bot (e.g. /w BotName hello) will not generate an AI response.

Reason: Whispers are strictly reserved for playerbot control commands (like follow, stay, init=auto). If Ollama intercepted whispers, the AI would try to carry on a conversation instead of letting you control their movement and gear!

To chat with a bot, always use local Say or group Party chat.

Server Features & Installed Modules

Our server runs several custom modules designed to adapt the core WotLK engine for a fun, rich cooperative and single-player progression experience. Below is a detailed reference of every modification currently installed:

⚔️ Detailed Server Modules Directory

1. Companion Bots (mod-playerbots) Active

Our primary server modification. It spawns hundreds of simulated active players throughout the world who chat, form groups, quest, and list items on the Auction House. You can summon your own custom companions via the control panel or in-game, group with them, clear dungeons together, and gear them up.

2. AI Ollama Chat (mod-ollama-chat) Active

Links companion bots to a local Large Language Model (Ollama) running on the host. When you speak to them in Say or Party chat, they generate context-aware, immersive replies matching their class, faction, and specific AI personality preset.

3. Autobalance (mod-autobalance) Active

Dynamically scales the health, damage output, armor, and combat parameters of dungeon and raid creatures. The scaling factor adjusts instantly based on the number of active human and bot characters currently in the instance, allowing anything from solo runs to standard full groups.

4. AHBot (mod-ahbot) Active

Simulates a live server economy. It runs in the background buying and selling trade goods, weapons, recipes, and resources. Our configuration links listing ownership to **Account ID 1 (Character GUID 1, Gnenlirgunk)**, ensuring a robust, constantly populated Auction House market.

5. Transmog (mod-transmog) Active

Allows players to visit Transmogrifier NPCs in major cities to modify the model visual appearance of their worn armor and weapons to match other items in their collection without losing stats.

6. Diablo-Style Random Enchants (mod-random-enchants) Active

Simulates action RPG loot mechanics. When equipment is dropped by monsters, looted from chests, or crafted by a profession, it has a random chance to roll bonus stats, scaling from minor attributes up to unique class spell modifiers.

7. AoE Looting System (mod-aoe-loot) Active

Enables AoE looting. Looting a single corpse will automatically aggregate all lootable items from all nearby defeated monster corpses in a 20-yard radius, saving significant clicks.

8. Mythic+ Dungeons (mod-mythic-plus) Active

Allows players to active key-based dungeon scaling. Interacting with the Keystone Pedestal at the start of any instance triggers increased boss health and damage along with weekly gameplay affixes (like Sanguine or Bolstering).

9. Solo LFG System (mod-solo-lfg) Active

Enables single players to search and register for Dungeon Finder pools without requiring a pre-made group. The system automatically fills gaps to allow progression.

10. All-Professions Simultaneous (mod-all-professions) Active

Bypasses the default client restriction limit of 2 primary professions. On OurAzeroth, players can learn **all 11 primary crafting/gathering professions** on a single character. Spawns custom trainers who teach skills for free.

11. All Races All Classes (mod-arac) Active

Completely removes traditional race/class restrictions. You can play a Gnome Paladin, a Tauren Rogue, or an Undead Druid. Requires the client-side `Patch-A.MPQ` to align client assets.

12. Account Mounts & Achievements (mod-account-wide) Active

Synchronizes all learned mounts, vanity pets, and completed achievements across all characters registered under the same account ID, allowing alts to share progression.

13. Item Upgrade Vendor (mod-item-upgrade) Active

Spawns upgrading vendors in Stormwind, Ironforge, and Orgrimmar who will raise the item level and stat weights of your gear in exchange for gold and currencies.

14. Phased Guild Housing (mod-guildhouse) Active

Upgraded from the legacy Lua version to the full phased C++ module. Guilds can purchase a private instance map from the recruiter (NPC 500030) and buy mailboxes, spirit healers, class trainers, portal hubs, and auctioneers inside their private zone.

15. TCG & Promotional Redeems (mod-tcg-vendors) Active

Enables redemption of promotional codes at vanity NPCs. In **Boss Drop Mode**, pre-generated codes drop in dungeon packages, which players read and hand to vendors (like Landro Longshot in Booty Bay) to unlock rare vanity mounts or pets.

16. Individual XP Rates (mod-individual-xp) Active

Enables custom experience sliders. Set your own rate between 1x and 10x using the .xp rate [num] command or by right-clicking the XP bar with the SetXpBar addon.

17. Group Quest Loot Sharing (mod-quest-loot-party) Active

Automatically shares dropped quest items. When a target drops a quest item, it is cloned and distributed to all party members in the area who have the quest active, bypassing the need to farm targets individually.

🏆 Challenge Modes (Opt-in Gameplay Rules)

The Challenge Modes module introduces custom gameplay rulesets to the server. These challenges are NOT realm-wide; instead, they are character-specific opt-ins. When you create a new Level 1 character, you will find a mysterious interactive object called the Shrine of Challenge (a glowing blue chalice object) in your starting area. If you interact with it at Level 1 before gaining any XP, you can choose to activate one or more of these rulesets for that character. The available modes are:

  • Hardcore Mode: If your character dies, you permanently become a ghost and can never be revived (one-life run).
  • Semi-Hardcore Mode: When you die, you lose all worn equipment and carried gold, but can still revive.
  • Ironman Mode: Enforces strict Ironman rules (wear only white/gray gear, no talents, no professions, no buffs, no groups, and permanent death on first defeat).
  • Self-Crafted Mode: You are only allowed to wear equipment that your character has personally crafted.
  • Item Quality Mode: You are only allowed to wear equipment of Normal (White) or Poor (Gray) quality.
  • Slow XP Modes: Reduces your experience gains to 0.5x (Slow) or 0.25x (Very Slow) for a slower level progression.
  • Quest XP Only: You gain experience points exclusively from completing quests; killing monsters yields zero XP.

🏹 Hunter Melee Weaving Disconnect Workaround

If you are playing a Hunter (like Eliphas), you may experience sudden disconnects to the login screen right when clicking Raptor Strike in melee range while Auto Shot is active. This is a known client-side combat state lock bug in the original 2008 WotLK client.

How to Fix in Your Client Settings

  1. Press Esc to open the Game Menu.
  2. Select Interface.
  3. Select the Combat category on the left side.
  4. Find the setting Auto Attack/Auto Shot:
    • Uncheck the box if it is currently enabled.
  5. Alternatively, ensure Stop Auto Attack is checked.
  6. Click Okay and test using Raptor Strike again in melee range.
