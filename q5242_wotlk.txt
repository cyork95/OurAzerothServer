By Ormriss (20,478 – 3·68·145) on 2006/12/09 (Patch 2.0.1)

Just did this with two shamans, level 57 and 53. It was very easy. Clear all the nearby mobs to prevent adds, then pile on Lord Fel'dan. If you have any spell interrupt abilities, use them as he starts to cast. He didn't seem to have much health. The two succubi only used melee attacks the entire fight, so with some quick healing and focusing on one at a time, they went down as fast as their lord.

Another note: they are not at the very back of Shadow Hold. Lord Banehollow, a level 59 elite demon, is at the very back. These three spawn earlier, in front of a two-sided ramp that leads into a larger area. We missed them the first time around because another party doing one leg of the Warlock epic mount quest had swept through earlier and the three mobs had not respawned. We looked around until we found Lord Banehollow, then started backtracking. By the time we came back to the ramp, the three mobs we needed had respawned (along with everything else nearby). Had we known to stay in that room, we could have had an even easier time of it, killing each mob as it spawned, rather than taking all three at once.

By 57846 (584 – 1·7) on 2007/07/02 (Patch 2.1.2)

Here's how I went about this relatively simple, time consuming but rewarding quest:

Make sure you have this and the quest to gather Trey's remains (You need to free the night elf from the cage from a random key drop. All the braziers can be extinguished during the run to get her gear, plus she can do some mild damage to any mobs giving you trouble. Once you've freed her and put out the fires, you'll get the two quests.)

At level 57, Shadow Hold is a push-over. Almost every mob, except for the larger Demons, are green. If you're a melee class, you'll cut through these guys like butter. Casters might want to take it slow and make sure your mana is full enough, and rogues or druids (cat form) can most likely stealth to the very bottom.

Start by clearing down to the place where the night elf captive found her gear. There will be five or so ooze creatures seperating the second half of the "dungeon". They are immune to nature damage, so druids and shamans (like I was) take caution.

After the ooze, you'll find more of the same casters and sword/shield mobs you fought earlier. If you can, hug the right wall and attack anything that attacks you. Otherwise, just slice through them until you reach a narrow tunnel.

Inside here you'll find the Succubus you need to kill for the Trey's remains quest. She can be pulled alone, and when she dies, a yellow ? will appear above the naked human on the altar. Pull the 4 casters, kill them and finish that quest.

Continue along down the route and a couple of mobs later you'll end up at Fel'dan and his guards. Clear the mobs nearby, and focus your efforts on Fel'dan. He should fall very easy if he's of yellow quest coloring to you. The two Succubus guards hit slow and not very hard at that. They cast a mind control spell, so be careful. They're levels 52 and 54, posing very little threat. Kill them, loot the head off Fel'dan, and either hearth out or start cutting your way back through. (I tried to just run out. ...didn't work.)

Best of luck!

From ThottbotBy Simon83uk on 2007/04/02 (Patch 2.0.10)

Subject: "If it's an orange quest, therer's a good chance you can still solo it..."
Not going to say "this was sooo easy blah blah", but straight down to my personal experiences with this quest:

- Co-ordinates have been given already, so won't go into that, read above if you need them.

- Well laid out comment someway near the top also explains major landmarks, simply a case of taking on the mobs one at a time, as theyre well-spaced out for the most part and can be pulled individually in 95% of cases.

- First major trouble-spot for me, was the big room with the 4 casters in middle (2 with minions), surrounded by an enforer, a felhunter and a legionnaire, and a succubus. BUT, for some reason, they all seem to be completely independant of each other, even the minions from their owners from my experiences. First one to pull should be the succubus, kill that near the stairs. then you'll want to kill one of the two casters at the front, shouldnt matter, but i started with left one, then right, again pulling them to the stairway and finishing them off there with no risk of aggroing others. Then I killed off the felhunter to the left, and then the legionnaire to the right. Then its a case of clearing one of the warlocks and their minion, and then the other one. Think i mentioned an enforcer, but this might have replaced the felhunter spawn on my way back out, but if it is there, same technique as rest of mobs in this room. Eventually they should all be clear and you can move on. As i said, they all seem to be in a trance, because i found an imp minion firing at me on the way back out, and the warlock wasnt doing anything, even when I killed the imp.

- Final challenge is obviously going to be the main bosses, but if you've got a pet, it should be a routine fight if youre used to dealing with a couple of mobs at once. With a warlock it's much simpler because you can banish one, have a minion fighting one, and then one for yourself. But anyway, there's 3 main bosses, the healer to the left, the standard to the right, and the main boss in the middle, levels 54, 52 and 57 respectively if memory serves me right. But before you kill those, theres a few patrolling mobs that you should take care of first, just to give you some breathing space. As long as youre careful, they shouldnt aggro the main bosses, so just dispatch the one straight ahead of you, and the ones to the right and left. dont worry about the one up the back of the stairs or in the corner, didnt go near them.

- The bosses themselves, I basically banished the healer (mana bar on them), had my minion (felguard in this instance, but voidwalker should do the trick) on the main boss, and I killed the standard succubus (level 52 non-healer). By the time I killed her, the other succubus was coming out of banish, so I finished her off (if your creature is low on health, you can always banish her again) and by then, my felguard had pretty much killed the main boss. The usual DoTs if possible should be applied, just to speed the process up.

Thats my personal experiences anyway, the succubus' didnt put any mind control spells on me, but each experience is different, you might be unlucky in that instance.

Hope this helps anyway, as I said, im not going to say it was easy, even if it wasnt as much of a challenge as I was expecting from an orange quest, but as long as you don't go in with a big head, or expecting a walkthrough, you should be fine

Just my experiences anyway, hope to be of help to someone.

Soloed as a lv54 Rogue just now. This quest is extremely easy for a Rogue thanks to stealth (Druids will have an equally easy time). The mobs are spread out just enough to where you can solo them one at a time with no real worries to yourself. If you find yourself with too much on your plate, you can always vanish and run.

An easy way to do this quest is to sap the boss then begin to kill off the two minions. Drop Salia by doing a good ol fashion stun lock (Cheap Shot -> Kidney Shot). Due to low health, she should drop just after those 10 seconds (poisons work wonders). From there, you can choose to face the other one head to head, or vanish and use the same strategy. Both of them should drop in enough time for you to stealth before the boss gets out of sap (in your vanish, you can always just reset the sap). Grab the head and stealth back out. Easy 13k experince. ^.^

By Charinna (4,188 – 15·41) on 2020/12/18 (Classic)

Here's how I did this as a level 55 druid (I was in bear form), though I think this technique works for any melee class.

Hint: There's a big ramp behind them.

1. I cleared the trash around the room and on the ramp behind.
2. I pulled all 3. He is ranged, the other two like melee.
3. Start killing one of them (I targeted 52 first but then switched to the 54)
4. Every time you see him stand still to cast, either run up the ramp if you're off the ramp, or jump down if you're on the ramp
5. The two succubi chase you. Kill one then the other while running around in circles on and off the ramp LOSing the poor guy
6. Now he has no friends left, so it's just the two of you

It was actually fun, and I don't think he got a single cast off before I killed his two friends just because I kept going in circles, a little up the ramp then jumping down.
